Hybrid text summarization is an approach that combines multiple techniques or methods to generate concise summaries of text documents. This method leverages the strengths of different summarization approaches, such as extractive and abstractive summarization, to produce more accurate and informative summaries.

In hybrid text summarization, extractive techniques involve selecting important sentences or phrases directly from the original text, usually based on criteria like relevance or importance scores computed using algorithms. These techniques are efficient and preserve the original wording, but they may not always capture the essence of the content effectively. On the other hand, abstractive techniques involve generating summaries by paraphrasing and rephrasing the original text, potentially introducing new words and sentence structures. Abstractive summarization can produce more human-like summaries and capture the main ideas more effectively, but it is often more challenging and requires advanced natural language understanding and generation capabilities.

By combining extractive and abstractive approaches, hybrid text summarization aims to overcome the limitations of each method while benefiting from their respective strengths. For instance, extractive methods can be used to identify important sentences or phrases, which are then rephrased or synthesized using abstractive techniques to create more coherent and informative summaries. Overall, hybrid text summarization offers a flexible and powerful approach to summarizing text documents, allowing for more accurate and comprehensive summaries that better capture the essence of the original content.
